About Me

I am currently a student at Washington University in St. Louis pursuing both a MS and BS in Computer Engineering. Before that I was a student at Rhodes College for a BS in Computer Science. I've done many research projects related to the intersection of people and robotics from safety, colaboration, and policiy perspectives. I have a strong interest in the hardware side of computing, and I'm excited to work on projects that combine both hardware and software skills.

At Rhodes College, I was involved in various extracurricular activities including the Vice President of the Gender and Sexuality Alliance, the social media chair of the Fencing Club, and the Secretary of the Women in STEM club. I was also induced into the Sigma Pi Sigma Physics Honor Society and the Upsilon Pi Epsilon Computer Science Honor Society. At WashU, I am involved in the Robotics Club, working on their robotics arm project named "Nimble". We are currently researching the utilization of VLAs (Vision Language Action) models to control the arm. At both Universities, I am also involved in the Orchestra, playing the French Horn.

For fun, I enjoy reading, playing the guitar, crochetting, and hiking.
